My Journey into dev...

The start...
I could never tell you my first set of code. I spoke computer language before I realized I could. The kid who dabbled in Myspace code and updated profiles for his friends, made PC adjustments through the command-line, and manually updated graffic cards to run PC games the smoothest. The "Wizard".

I knew I wanted to build/create amazing things that amazed people but wasn't aware of too many careers that fulfilled that.

The step...
In high school, My art/computer teacher introduced me to the idea of being an architect.

That was probably because most of my projects were abstract designs and complex building structures.

Finding myself...
After a few weeks went by, and doing a little research, I was sold on being an architect. My curiousity peaked and I decided I would go to school to be an Architect. The day I decided to become a software engineer, I was on Khan Academy looking for math help and I glanced a section for programming and decided to dabble.

I completed the coursework for JavaScript animation and was hooked! This was architecture on demand! Without the need for a lab or resources. That opened up a world of online learning for me in which I continued to indulge. I continued learning by taking a course called “Intro to Computer Science” on a platforms like CodeAcademy and Udacity. That alone inspired me enough to change to a Computer Science major.

The Journey Begins...
I’m consistently reading over docs and taking online challenges expanding my knowledge of development. I did a Bootcamp fellowship program called The Knowledge House (Summer 2018) that really helped me develop my career and technical skills where my Python history really help me excel in aquiring HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Post program, I furthered my web development educating myself on various tools (ie. PHP, Angular, Bulma, etc). I developed my strongest language (Python) through coursework and now can say I’m at a confident level.

Joining the tech field, I decided to be a student for the rest of my life. The possibilities are endless. My job as an engineer is to help forward and innovate different ideas to contribute in the development of our culture for this generation and the next.
